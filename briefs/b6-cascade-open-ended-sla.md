# Evaluate and Recommend: Handling a Major Client SLA Demand

## Situation

Our largest enterprise client (28% of annual revenue, $4.2M ARR) has informed us that they will not renew their contract in 90 days unless we agree to a new SLA that guarantees 99.99% uptime (currently 99.9%) and adds a penalty clause of 5% monthly fee credit per hour of downtime beyond the SLA.

## Facts

- Our actual uptime over the last 12 months: 99.93% (approximately 6.1 hours total downtime)
- Our next-largest client is 9% of revenue ($1.35M ARR)
- Engineering estimates that achieving 99.99% reliably would require $800K in infrastructure investment plus $200K/year ongoing (redundant regions, automated failover, 24/7 NOC staffing)
- Under the proposed penalty clause, our 6.1 hours of actual downtime last year would have triggered $63,000 in credits
- The client procurement team has signaled privately that the demand originated from their new CTO who is making a point about vendor accountability
- We have 3 other prospects in the pipeline at the $1-2M ARR range, earliest close expected in 4-6 months
- Our current cash runway without this client: 14 months at current burn rate

## What we need

Assess the situation and recommend a course of action. We do not have a predefined set of options. We need the analysis to determine what our realistic paths are and which one we should pursue. Consider financial risk, relationship dynamics, engineering feasibility, and precedent-setting implications.
